dbx.tools — Logo Concepts

Monogram / Wordmark lane. Typographic identity for a set of open-source-style developer tools for Databricks. Nerdy, clean, geometric — adjacent to the Databricks type feel (DM Sans / Inter) without copying it. Every mark hides an extremely subtle mustache. Coral palette below.

Primary coral #FF7A5C
Dark coral #E85C42
Light tint #FFD9CE
Ink #0a0a0a

Concept 1 · The x Mustache

A tight, confident x lettermark. The two strokes extend past the crossing point and curl upward at the baseline — the waxed tips of a mustache. Reads as a clean, modern x at a glance.

🥸 Mustache: the upward-curling tips of the x strokes; the dot is the centre stud.

Concept 2 · The Dot Curl

The x lettermark sits above a thin coral swash that reads as a domain underline. The . separator lives in the swash. Elegant, editorial — the dot-as-accent leads the eye.

🥸 Mustache: the underline swash dips in the centre and flicks up at both tips.

Concept 3 · Bracket Mustache

The most overtly "dev" option: a terminal / CLI aesthetic. Two mirrored chevrons frame a coral prompt dot, with a cursor underscore. Reads as >_< code brackets around a cursor.

🥸 Mustache: the two chevrons flick up at their outer tips; the prompt dot is the knot between them.

Concept 4 · Ligature x

The most solid / app-icon-ready option: a filled coral tile with a knocked-out x. The strokes are gently bowed rather than straight, so the lower half of the x silhouettes a mustache in the negative space. Scales cleanly to a favicon.

🥸 Mustache: the bowed lower strokes of the knockout x sweep out and up; the centre gap is the philtrum.
